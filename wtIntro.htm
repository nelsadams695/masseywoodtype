Roy Gurney Wood Type Collection

Eye-catching, iconoclastic, grotesque, ornamented, and instantly recognizable  wood display faces were the raw material for advertising, posters, and public announcements throughout the nineteenth century and into the twentieth. The Roy Gurney Collection at the Robertson Davies Library consists of 356 fonts of wood printing type dating from the 1850s to the 1960s, primarily of pantograph-and-router-cut endgrain hardwood but representing a range of manufacturing methods including die-cut, hand-cut, and veneer types and ornamental borders.
  This index is intended to facilitate access to the collection. We continue to develop the index, working to print specimens of each font of type, and to identify each one by linking it to existing specimen books. It is hoped that as more institutions, presses, and individuals sort and catalogue what wood type they have, a network of similar indexes will allow printers and scholars to generate more knowledge about wood type, to identify more faces, and to better preserve this important aspect of printing history.
  The Roy Gurney collection is a valuable resource for anyone interested in the development, design, manufacture, and use of wood type over the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries  including designers, printers, historians of type design, students, and general interest users. The collection is supported by a range of wood type specimen books and the print collections of the library.
