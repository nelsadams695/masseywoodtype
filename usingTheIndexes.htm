Using the Indexes

Welcome to the Robertson Davies Library Wood and Metal Type Indexes.
  In whatever column you are currently sorting by, the list of items has a white background, and the column-header has an orange background. If you want to sort by the contents of a different column, click on that column's header.
  Not every column needs to be displayed at all times. You can limit your display to show only the information that is relevant to you by using the section called "show or hide columns". If a column is currently showing, it will appear in black in this section. If it is not showing, it will appear in grey. If you want to hide a column that is currently showing, click on its name in the "show or hide columns". If you want to show a column which is currently hidden, click on its name in the "show or hide columns" section.
  An example from the Wood Type Index: If you were interested in seeing what die-cut types are present within the collection, sorting by "Manufacturing method" would group the items in the collection using manufacturing method as the primary criterion. Ensure that this column is displaying by making "method of manufacture" black in the "show or hide columns" section. Next, click on the "Mfg. Method" column header. The header will turn orange, the column will turn white, and the items will be arranged by manufacturing method. Scroll down until you see "die cut", and there you have every font of type or ornamental piece which was made using the die cut method.
